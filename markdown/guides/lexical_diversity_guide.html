Lexical Diversity Metrics

Lexical diversity measures vocabulary richness by quantifying the relationship between unique words (types) and total words (tokens).

Metrics

  • MTLD: Measure of Textual Lexical Diversity - most stable, text-length independent
  • MATTR: Moving Average TTR - highly stable across all text lengths
  • MSTTR: Mean Segmental TTR - averages TTR across fixed segments
  • TTR: Type-Token Ratio - simple but sensitive to text length
  • CTTR: Corrected TTR - partially addresses length sensitivity
  • Maas: Measures relative vocabulary growth
  • Yule's K: Classic vocabulary richness measure
  • Simpson's D: Diversity using frequency distributions

Usage

  • Use MTLD or MATTR for reliable, length-independent analysis
  • Avoid TTR as primary measure - highly sensitive to text length
  • Higher values indicate richer vocabulary
