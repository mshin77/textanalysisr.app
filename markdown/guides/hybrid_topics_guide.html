Hybrid Topic Modeling

Combines the strengths of both STM and embedding-based approaches. Uses transformer embeddings for semantic understanding while maintaining STM's ability to model covariate relationships and provide probabilistic topic assignments.

Best For

  • Best of both worlds: Need semantic coherence AND covariate modeling
  • Complex research: Testing hypotheses about how metadata affects semantically-defined topics
  • Validation: Compare and validate findings across different methodological approaches
  • Robust analysis: Want confidence that topics aren't artifacts of a single method

Usage

Initializes STM with semantic embeddings rather than traditional bag-of-words, maintains interpretability of probabilistic models, incorporates covariate effects, and provides cross-validation between methods. Computationally intensive but offers methodological rigor for high-stakes research.

Quality Metrics

The hybrid model computes several quality metrics to assess model fit:

  • Semantic Coherence: Measures how often top words co-occur in documents (higher is better)
  • Exclusivity: Measures how unique words are to each topic (higher is better)
  • Silhouette Score: Measures cluster separation for embedding topics (-1 to 1, higher is better)
  • Alignment Score: Agreement between STM and embedding topic assignments
  • Adjusted Rand Index: Clustering agreement corrected for chance
