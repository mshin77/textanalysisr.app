Part-of-Speech Tagging

Part-of-speech (POS) tagging identifies the grammatical category of each word. spaCy provides both Universal (coarse) and Detailed (fine-grained) tags.

Universal POS Tags

Open Class Words (content words)

  • NOUN – Noun
  • VERB – Verb
  • ADJ – Adjective
  • ADV – Adverb
  • PROPN – Proper Noun
  • INTJ – Interjection (oh, wow, oops)

Closed Class Words (function words)

  • DET – Determiner (the, a, this)
  • ADP – Adposition: prepositions and postpositions (in, to, during)
  • PRON – Pronoun
  • NUM – Numeral
  • AUX – Auxiliary
  • CCONJ – Coordinating Conjunction (and, or, but)
  • SCONJ – Subordinating Conjunction (if, while, because)
  • PART – Particle (not, 's, to in infinitives)

Other

  • PUNCT – Punctuation
  • SYM – Symbol ($, %, @)
  • X – Other (foreign words, typos)

Detailed Tags (Penn Treebank)

Fine-grained English-specific tags

  • NN – Noun, Singular; NNS – Noun, Plural
  • NNP – Proper Noun, Singular; NNPS – Proper Noun, Plural
  • VB – Verb, Base Form
  • VBD – Verb, Past Tense
  • VBG – Verb, Gerund/Present Participle
  • VBN – Verb, Past Participle
  • VBP – Verb, Non-3rd Person Singular Present
  • VBZ – Verb, 3rd Person Singular Present
  • JJ – Adjective; JJR – Adjective, Comparative; JJS – Adjective, Superlative
  • RB – Adverb; RBR – Adverb, Comparative; RBS – Adverb, Superlative
  • PRP – Personal Pronoun; PRP$ – Possessive Pronoun
  • DT – Determiner
  • IN – Preposition or Subordinating Conjunction
  • CC – Coordinating Conjunction
  • CD – Cardinal Number
