Dependency Parsing

Dependency parsing identifies grammatical relationships between words. Each word connects to a "head" word through a dependency relation.

Core Arguments

  • nsubj – Nominal Subject
  • obj – Object (direct object)
  • iobj – Indirect Object
  • csubj – Clausal Subject ("What she said surprised me")
  • ccomp – Clausal Complement ("He said that he would come")
  • xcomp – Open Clausal Complement: clause without its own subject ("I want to go")

Modifiers

  • amod – Adjectival Modifier
  • advmod – Adverbial Modifier
  • nmod – Nominal Modifier
  • nummod – Numeric Modifier
  • acl – Adnominal Clause: clause modifying a noun ("the man who arrived")
  • advcl – Adverbial Clause Modifier ("If it rains, we stay")

Function Words

  • det – Determiner
  • case – Case Marking: adpositions, postpositions
  • aux – Auxiliary
  • cop – Copula (linking verb like "is")
  • mark – Marker: subordinating conjunction introducing a clause

Coordination & Compounding

  • conj – Conjunct
  • cc – Coordinating Conjunction
  • compound – Compound ("ice cream", "New York")
  • flat – Flat Expression: names without internal structure ("John Smith")
  • fixed – Fixed Multiword Expression ("in spite of")

Other Relations

  • root – Root: head of the sentence
  • appos – Appositional Modifier ("my friend, John")
  • obl – Oblique Nominal: non-core nominal argument
  • vocative – Vocative: direct address ("John, come here")
  • expl – Expletive ("There is a problem")
  • punct – Punctuation
  • dep – Unspecified Dependency
